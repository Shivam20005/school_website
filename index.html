George Orwell wrote his last novel, "1984", in 1948, and it was published a year later in 1949. It is renouwned as on of the greatest dystopias in English literature. He writes 1984 as a warning to the Western world about the dangers of totalitarian states, and being written in a post world war II period the message was ever-relevant. 1984 was his last novel, he had also written his satircal allegory "Animal Farm", which is also another modern classic. Orwell has written a few more novels, along with a selection of essays. He is arguably one of the best writers in the English language, and definitely one of the influential people of the 20th century. Words from his novel 1984 such as "Big Brother" and "Doublethink" have entered our common parlance.
